Got more questions?

What is DurJam?

DurJam is an online game jam run by Durham University Computing Society (CompSoc) from 26th to 30th August 2020.

What is a game jam?

A game jam is an invention marathon where teams come together to rapidly develop a game. Participants learn, develop, and innovate together from around the world.

Who can attend?

DurJam is open to current students and those who have graduated in the last year.

How do I sign up?

Fill out our Typeform to register for the event and then join the Discord. Make sure youâ€™ve registered for the event so youâ€™re eligible for prizes!

How big can my team be?

Please keep your teams to a maximum of 5 members. We can only offer prizes to the first 5 members of a team.

How do we work remotely?

We recommend setting up a group chat to communicate with your team. It can also be helpful to video call and collaborate on code in real-time (codeshare.io and VS Code Live Share).

Who owns what I make?

The Intellectual Property of your work remains with you.

How do I submit?

You should submit your project on our Devpost. Only one member of your team needs to submit the project, then they can add you as a collaborator to ensure you are eligible for prizes.

Is there a Code of Conduct?

DurJam follows the MLH Code of Conduct and standard competition rules.
